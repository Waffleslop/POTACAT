## 📻 SSTV: the decoder grew up

We root-caused why some of you stopped decoding SSTV in late June (a Flex speaker-mute change silently killed the DAX audio tap in v1.8.15–1.8.17 — fixed since v1.9.0) and then went after the reasons MMSSTV still felt more reliable. It shouldn't anymore:

- **AFC — POTACAT now decodes stations MMSSTV can't.** The decoder retunes itself to off-frequency signals automatically (±300 Hz). Real 14.230 operation routinely has stations 100–200 Hz apart in tuning; the old decoder went silently deaf past ~75 Hz. MMSSTV has no AFC at all — its users tune by eye against spectrum markers.
- **Joins transmissions already in progress.** Missed the VIS header (QSB, tuned in late)? The decoder now identifies the mode from the sync-pulse rhythm and starts mid-picture, like MMSSTV's no-VIS start.
- **Weak signals show you something.** Borderline decodes used to be silently discarded; now they display with an orange **⚠ WEAK** badge (and stay out of your auto-saved gallery). Noise is still rejected — the badge is for real, marginal pictures.
- **↻ Redecode**: the last transmission stays in an audio buffer — adjust the slant slider and redecode it for real (MMSSTV's "replay from RX buffer"), not just a cosmetic shear.
- **✕ NO RX AUDIO** badge: when no audio is reaching the decoder (dead DAX route, wrong input, muted slice), you'll know in seconds instead of wondering why nothing decodes — and if the direct radio stream dies, the soundcard input now takes over automatically within seconds.

## 🎆 Special-event and compound callsigns work on FT8

Anything with a slash (`PJ4/K1ABC`) or an unusual shape (`GB13COL`, `TM13COL`) travels as a hashed `<call>` in FT8's 77 bits — and every reply JTCAT sent one **silently never transmitted**. Fixed end to end: reports hash their call, the final RR73/73 carries it in full, bare no-grid replies from nonstandard stations are understood, and `<...>` resolves automatically once their full call is heard. (13 Colonies' K2A–K2M were always ordinary calls — it's the GB13COL/TM13COL bonus stations this unlocks.)

## 🎪 Events, connected end to end

- Working a tracked event station (13 Colonies, America250) **stamps the event into the QSO record** — comment tag plus ADIF fields — so your log proves your certificate claim.
- **"Stamp N past QSOs"**: one click backfills the event into matching contacts you made before this release, then a clean per-event ADIF export for your submission. When an event finishes, its board shows the summary ("worked 12 of 13 — 9 FT8").
- New **Event** filter in JTCAT shows only stations you still need, and auto-answer now **prefers an event station you need over a stronger generic caller**.
- Event banners **retire themselves** when an event ends — no more restart required (and countdowns flip to LIVE on time). The event catalog now also updates live from potacat.com, so new events appear without an app update.
- The Contests view and the events system finally know about each other: the 13 Colonies row carries a live "◉ Tracked — 8/13 worked" chip that opens your board.

## 🎛️ Flex: firmware noise filters SmartSDR-style clients don't expose

For 8000-series/Aurora firmware: **NRL** (LMS adaptive), **NRS** (spectral subtraction), **RNN** (neural-net, voice-trained), and **NRF** noise reduction — plus **WNB** wideband noise blanking on *all* Flex models and RF gain in radio-native dB steps. POTACAT drives these over its own API connection, so they work with SmartSDR open, AetherSDR open, or no GUI client at all. NRL validated on the 8600M.

## 📱 ECHOCAT FT8 replies fixed

Tapping a station that answered your CQ transmitted your grid instead of their signal report — the phone knew the right message; the desktop dropped it in transit (since April, masked because QSOs still completed with an extra cycle). Every directed-decode tap now opens with the correct message, and the desktop re-checks each tap against the raw decode so a stale phone app can never cause it again.

## ⚡ JTCAT quality-of-life

- **Skip Grid**: answer CQs with your signal report instead of your grid (WSJT-X's "skip Tx1") — one full cycle shorter each way.
- **Hound mode**: work FT8 DXpeditions properly — calls above 1000 Hz, auto-QSY to the fox for the report, fox dual-messages understood, logs on RR73 with no wasted 73. (Classic Fox/Hound; SuperFox not yet.)

- **Already-worked warning**: double-clicking a station you've worked pops a band/mode-aware notice — and calls anyway.

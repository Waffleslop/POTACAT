Work special-event stations, shorten your QSOs, and hunt DXpeditions — JTCAT closes the gap with WSJT-X.

## 🎆 Special-event and compound callsigns finally work

FT8 can only fit a "normal" callsign in its 77 bits — anything with a slash (`PJ4/K1ABC`, `K3SBP/7`) or an unusual shape (`GB13COL`, `TM13COL`, `YW18FIFA`) travels as a **hash**, shown in angle brackets like `<GB13COL>`. JTCAT could *decode* those stations, but every reply you sent them **silently never transmitted** — the encoder rejected the message and nothing went out while the screen said otherwise.

That's fixed, end to end:

- Replies to nonstandard calls now encode correctly — reports hash their call (`<GB13COL> K3SBP -08`), and the final RR73/73 goes out as the special full-call message so everyone else on frequency learns who `<...>` actually is. Exactly the message shapes WSJT-X generates.
- When a nonstandard station answers **your** CQ (their reply can't carry a grid — there's no room), JTCAT now recognizes the bare two-call reply and runs the QSO instead of ignoring them.
- `<...>` means you haven't copied the station's full call yet — wait for their next CQ and JTCAT resolves it automatically.
- 13 Colonies note: the K2A–K2M stations were always fine (single-letter suffixes are ordinary calls) — it's the **GB13COL / TM13COL bonus stations** this unlocks.

## ⚡ Skip Grid — shorter QSOs

A new **Skip Grid** toggle in JTCAT answers a CQ with your signal report instead of your grid — `A1BC K3SBP -11` — the same trick as WSJT-X's "skip Tx1". Each QSO gets one full cycle shorter in each direction: 30 seconds saved on FT8, 15 on FT4. (JTCAT has understood *receiving* these compressed replies since v1.8; now it can send them too.)

## 🦊 Hound mode — work DXpeditions from JTCAT

DXpeditions running FT8 **Fox/Hound** mode follow special rules, and JTCAT now plays by them. Turn on the new **Hound** toggle, tune the DXpedition's published frequency, and double-click the fox:

- Your call goes out **above 1000 Hz** (the fox ignores anything lower), grid included, addressed to the fox's base call.
- The fox transmits **paired messages** working two hounds at once (`K1ABC RR73; W9XYZ <KH1/KH7Z> -08`) — JTCAT now decodes these and reads only *your* half, so someone else's RR73 can't close your QSO.
- When the fox answers you, your transmitter **automatically QSYs down to the fox's frequency** for the R-report — the handshake that actually completes the contact.
- On RR73 the QSO logs and TX stops immediately. No 73 courtesy — the fox has already moved on, and the pileup doesn't need your extra slot.

Heads up: this is the classic Fox/Hound protocol. **SuperFox** (the new verified single-waveform mode some 2025+ DXpeditions run) uses a different transmission format and isn't supported yet.

## ⌨️ Type your own TX message

Click the TX message readout in JTCAT and type anything — a 13-character free-text ("TNX JOE 73"), a directed call, whatever you need. The message is validated by the **real encoder** before it's accepted, so if JTCAT takes it, it will transmit; if it can't encode, you get a red border and the reason instead of silent dead air. Setting a message never keys the radio — Enable TX stays a deliberate, separate click.

Settings that match your radio, an FT4 receive fix, and logging polish.

## 🎛️ Settings that match your radio

An IC-7300 owner opened audio settings and found FlexRadio DAX options staring back. No more — POTACAT now scopes what you see to the rig you've defined:

- The **audio source picker only offers what your radio can actually do**. Flex rigs see Flex Direct + DAX; Icom Network (RS-BA1) rigs see network audio; everyone else sees their soundcard devices and nothing Flex-flavored at all.
- **Audio source is now saved per rig** and follows a rig switch. Before, switching from a Flex to another radio silently kept the Flex audio path — and JTCAT/SSTV went quiet until you found the dropdown.
- Flex-only controls (JTCAT **multi-slice**, TunerGenius/Antenna Genius accessories, the WinKeyer Flex-sidetone mute) now stay out of sight unless a Flex rig is defined — on desktop and on the phone.

## 📻 FT4 receive actually decodes now

An off-by-a-buffer bug meant the decoder's candidate scan never reached where FT4 signals sit in the capture window — **FT4 RX decoded nothing, ever**. FT8 dodged it by luck of its longer symbol time. Fixed; FT4 hears again.

## 🌐 World Radio League: your park number can't vanish

If you turned off v1.9.4's auto park/club comment tags, the hunted park stopped appearing anywhere in WRL — their Cat Control app ignores the dedicated park fields POTACAT sends and only keeps the comment text. The WRL packet now always carries a short `[POTA US-1234]` tag in its comment, regardless of the toggle, so the park survives; your own logbook keeps the clean comment you typed. (WRL packets also carry a unique contact ID + real seconds now, so same-minute multi-op QSOs stop collapsing into one.)

## 🛠️ Also in this release

- **K4 Remote**: FT8 and SSTV audio feeds from the K4's network stream never engaged due to a dead setting check — fixed.
- **SSB-over-DATA** PTT restore re-anchors to the pre-PTT dial, not a mid-transmit poll.
- **JTCAT** warns when the captured default audio input doesn't look like a radio (webcam mic, etc.).
- **ECHOCAT**: the TLS certificate auto-reissues when your Tailscale tailnet hostname changes (fixes iOS pairing after a tailnet rename).
- The logbook pop-out self-heals if it ever opens blank.
- The update banner no longer doubles the version in release titles.
- Release installers are now built with a pinned, reproducible toolchain, and a Windows validation build lets us hand out test installers before tagging.

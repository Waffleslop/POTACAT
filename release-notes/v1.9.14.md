A crash fix for CW text-send on Yaesu rigs that key through a CW Key Port, plus a fix for FT-710 transmitting at zero watts in JTCAT after opening ECHOCAT.

## CW text-send no longer crashes the app

On rigs configured to key CW through a dedicated CW Key Port on a serial adapter that can't be pin-controlled directly (common on Linux and Android USB adapters), POTACAT keys the port through a small Python helper. Sending CW text this way could crash the whole app: the text was piped to the helper over a stream that had no error handling, so if the helper exited early — a momentarily busy port, a missing dependency — the app went down instead of logging the problem. This surfaced right after selecting the FT-891 model, which is the first Yaesu that routes CW text through the key port.

- The CW text is now handed to the helper without that fragile stream, so the failure mode is gone entirely.
- CW text-send is wrapped so that no keying backend can ever take the app down again — a failed send logs a clear line instead.
- When the helper does fail, the log now names the actual reason (missing pyserial, busy port) so a rig that still won't key is diagnosable at a glance.

## FT-710 keying at zero watts in JTCAT after opening ECHOCAT

An FT-710 could key on CAT but put out no power in JTCAT specifically — voice and WSJT-X were fine — after a mobile device opened ECHOCAT. JTCAT's FT8 transmit audio and ECHOCAT's remote audio share one output-device setting, and connecting a mobile client could overwrite it with a Windows "Default"/"Communications" role device that drifts off the radio's USB codec, so the tones never reached the rig.

- POTACAT now refuses to store those Windows role devices as the transmit output, keeping the last known-good real device and logging why.
- The chosen device is mirrored onto the active rig so the global and per-rig copies can't diverge across a rig switch or restart.
- At transmit time, POTACAT warns loudly if the resolved output is still a role device — a catch-all for any older saved value.

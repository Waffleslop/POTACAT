Follow-up to the 13 Colonies Release — logging polish and remote-operation fixes.

## 📝 Your comment field, your call

POTACAT has always stamped logged QSOs' COMMENT field with the park details (`[POTA US-1234 US-GA Sweetwater Creek]`) and CW club tags. Handy for some, unwanted for others — especially N3FJP users who'd rather the comment stay exactly what they typed. Now it's a choice:

- New **"Auto park/club tags in comment"** checkbox in logging settings (on by default — nothing changes unless you turn it off). Off, the COMMENT field carries only what you type; the park still goes to `SIG_INFO`/`POTA_REF` for your logbook either way.
- Fixed a bug where **phone-logged POTA QSOs got the park tag twice** (`[POTA US-1234] note [POTA US-1234 US-GA …]`). One clean tag now, whichever device you log from.

## 📱 Steadier ECHOCAT with big logbooks

The desktop used to send your **entire QSO log to the phone in one giant message** on every connect — at several thousand QSOs that's many megabytes per reconnect, and big logs could crash the phone's connection in a loop. The log now transfers in small chunks (with updated phone app), and older phone versions get a capped payload instead of a connection-killer.

## ⌨️ CW paddle no longer dead-keys Yaesu/Kenwood serial rigs

Using the phone's paddle or straight key on a Yaesu (FT-991/FTdx10/FTdx101…) or Kenwood over serial CAT used to **key the transmitter with no CW output and no sidetone** — the radio went into transmit, silently. POTACAT now refuses that dead route, tells you why, and points at the fix (the rig's second USB COM port with PC KEYING = DTR, set as the CW Key Port). The phone app grays out the key button with the same guidance instead of silently dead-keying.

## 🛠️ Also in this release

- The phone learns CW-paddle availability at connect time (with the specific reason), not after the first wasted key press.
- TQSL/LoTW upload integration is scoped and planned — coming in a future release.

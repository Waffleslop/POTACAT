# SmartSDR Direct, End to End

Headline: "SmartSDR Direct" now works for *everything* — FT8 decodes, the FT8 waterfall (main window and pop-out), and the ECHOCAT audio bridge all run off the radio's direct VITA-49 stream instead of a Windows DAX device. Plus: the DAX stream auto-recovers when it stalls after TX, the audio-health detector stopped false-firing, and an FT8 QSO no longer dead-ends when the station you called answers someone else.

If you run a Flex on "SmartSDR Direct," this is the build where that setting finally means what it says.

## SmartSDR Direct now drives FT8 — decodes *and* waterfall

The "SmartSDR Direct" audio source was only ever wired to the ECHOCAT phone bridge. FT8/JTCAT ignored it completely and always captured the Windows `DAX Audio RX 1` device. On a SmartSDR Direct setup that Windows device is frequently silent — the radio routes audio to POTACAT's own direct VITA-49 subscription, and the DAX *program* feeding the Windows device may not have the channel running. Result: FT8 sat at zero decodes with a blank waterfall, even though the audio was right there.

v1.5.22 routes the VITA-49 stream into FT8:

- **Decodes** — the direct dax_rx audio (the same stream the ECHOCAT bridge already uses) is downsampled to the FT8 engine's rate and fed straight to the decoder. The renderer's Windows-device capture is bypassed so it can't double-feed.
- **Waterfall** — the raw VITA-49 frames are forwarded to whichever JTCAT renderer is live (main window or pop-out). Each builds a synthetic audio stream from them and runs its normal gain → analyser → waterfall pipeline unchanged. The waterfall renders from real audio now, in both the embedded view and the pop-out.

Net effect: on "SmartSDR Direct," the Windows DAX device is fully bypassed for JTCAT — exactly what that setting should have meant. No DAX program channel configuration required.

## DAX stream auto-recovers after a stall

POTACAT's direct VITA-49 subscription had only a one-shot startup watchdog — it confirmed audio *arrived* once, then never checked again. If the stream stalled mid-session (the Flex mutes slice RX during transmit, and the dax_rx stream often doesn't resume on its own at the TX→RX edge), the ECHOCAT audio bridge went silent with no recovery short of a full SmartSDR reconnect. Neither the iOS "Audio" toggle nor "Restart audio bridge" rebuilt it.

Now:

- A continuous liveness check watches the stream. If frames stop for more than a few seconds *while the rig is in RX*, the desktop automatically re-subscribes. A stall *during* TX is expected (slice RX is muted) and ignored — the check keeps watching, so a genuinely-dead stream still surfaces and recovers once TX ends.
- "Restart audio bridge" now rebuilds the VITA-49 subscriber too, not just the WebRTC window — so the manual recovery path actually works for SmartSDR Direct users.

## Audio-health detector stopped false-firing

The audio-health monitor was watching the wrong stream. On SmartSDR Direct (or with WebSDR audio active) the WebRTC bridge carries a synthetic stream, but the health detector was pinned to the original microphone/device capture — which is often silent on those setups. It saw silence, declared the bridge dead, and kicked off a restart loop: audio cutting out every few seconds until the circuit breaker gave up. The detector now follows whichever stream the bridge is actually sending, across every source swap.

## FT8: no more dead-end when the station answers someone else

You tap a CQ, POTACAT sends your reply — and the station comes back to a *different* caller. Previously POTACAT just aborted the QSO, with no clear indication of why.

Now POTACAT **holds** instead. A new "waiting" state:

- Keeps the QSO alive, TX paused, slot timing locked.
- Watches for the station to come free — their next CQ, or them signing off the QSO they're in.
- When they're free, **automatically re-sends your reply** — no re-tap needed.
- Times out after about 3 minutes if they never return.

The desktop shows a clear flag while it waits: **⏸ KE4QEG is working W2HTS — waiting to reply**, with the QSO tracker held at the reply step. (The ECHOCAT iOS app will show this once a matching app update ships; the hold-and-re-arm behavior already works on the iOS path today.)

```
sudo xattr -dr com.apple.quarantine /Applications/POTACAT.app
```
